--- TWEET 1/5 (HOOK) ---
We spent months watching WeChat group admins burn hours scanning for scams.

Then we built WeClaw.

Here's why it exists, how it works, and why it's 100% open source 🧵

--- TWEET 2/5 ---
The problem: Manual moderation doesn't scale.

Admins told us 1–3 hrs/week just scanning. Scammers exploit trust. By the time you notice, damage is done.

We wanted AI to *see* what admins see—and flag threats before they spread.

--- TWEET 3/5 ---
The approach: Vision-based automation. No APIs. No reverse engineering.

WeClaw uses screenshots + LLM to read the WeChat UI like a human would. Built on CUA (Computer Use Agents). Find → Click → Verify. You approve. It executes.

Your machine. Your control.

--- TWEET 4/5 ---
Why open source?

• Transparency — you see every line
• Extensible — add skills, tune behavior, no vendor lock-in
• Community — we build what users ask for

Reply gen, friend verification, more — coming from your feedback.
