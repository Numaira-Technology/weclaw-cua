Title: 3 ways scam artists target WeChat groups (and how to spot them)

---

Copy (paste into LinkedIn post):

Scam artists love WeChat groups. High trust. High volume. Hard to moderate.

Here are 3 patterns we see again and again—and how to spot them before damage is done.

1. THE "HELPFUL" STRANGER
Joins as a new member, stays quiet for a few days, then offers "exclusive deals," "investment tips," or "customer service." Often impersonates brands or official support.
→ Red flag: Unsolicited DMs, links to external sites, pressure to act fast.

2. THE FAKE ACCOUNT FARM
Multiple accounts join over time, upvote each other, and create fake social proof. Then one "breaks character" to pitch a scheme.
→ Red flag: New accounts with minimal history, similar profile pics or bios, coordinated posting.

3. THE PHISHING LINK
Messages with "verify your account," "claim your prize," or "check this out" — leading to credential theft or malware.
→ Red flag: Shortened URLs, mismatched domains, urgency language.
