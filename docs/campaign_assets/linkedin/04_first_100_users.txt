WeClaw hit 100 users this week. Here's what we learned.

1. Moderation is a time sink.
Group admins told us they spend 1–3 hours a week just scanning for scams. WeClaw cut that to minutes. The "human confirm before remove" flow gave them confidence without giving up control.

2. Trust is earned, not assumed.
"AI will remove people" made folks nervous. Showing the plan before execution—and letting them click Cancel—changed the conversation. Transparency builds adoption.

3. Edge cases are everywhere.
Different group types (family, business, community) have different norms. One config doesn't fit all. We're adding skill customization and community-contributed playbooks.

4. Open source = faster feedback.
Bugs and feature requests came in via GitHub. Contributors proposed improvements we hadn't thought of. Building in public is working.

What's next: reply generation, friend request verification, and more use cases driven by your feedback.

Thanks to everyone who tried WeClaw, starred the repo, and told us what they need. Onward.
